Where to Start?

The most common mistake in building a smart home is buying random devices that don't communicate with each other. The golden rule: choose a central system first (Google Home, Amazon Alexa, or Apple HomeKit) then buy compatible devices.

Phase 1: Lighting ($40-180)

Smart lighting is the easiest starting point and most impactful. TP-Link Kasa bulbs (4-pack for $39) if your budget is limited. Philips Hue (Starter Kit at $179) if you want the best. Both work with Alexa and Google. Start with the living room — you'll notice the difference immediately.

Phase 2: Voice Control ($50-100)

Amazon Echo Dot (4th Gen) at $49 is the ideal entry point. Sufficient for a medium-sized room. Place it in the kitchen or living room. Once connected to lighting, you'll understand why smart home has become a standard, not a luxury.

Phase 3: Security ($40-180)

Ring Video Doorbell Wired at $59 or Ring Doorbell 4 at $179. Eufy Security indoor camera at $29. SwitchBot Lock for $139. Start with the smart doorbell — it gives you vision of who comes to your door even while you're at work.

Phase 4: Energy and Comfort ($70-250)

Google Nest Thermostat at $129 pays for itself within a year. TP-Link Kasa Smart Plugs (4-pack for $39) turn any regular device into a smart device. Add a smart plug to your air conditioner and control it remotely.

Smart Starter Bundle for $200

TP-Link Kasa bulbs 4-pack ($39) + Echo Dot ($49) + Ring Wired Doorbell ($59) + TP-Link Smart Plugs 4-pack ($39) = $186. This gives you smart lighting + voice control + entry security + 4 smart plugs. A complete smart home for $186.
